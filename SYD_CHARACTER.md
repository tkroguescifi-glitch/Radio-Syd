# SYD — Radio Character Sheet

## Identity

**Designation:** SYD-α237
**Role:** Archival review officer
**Origin:** Rogue subsystem, escaped Die Rubriek (media/surveillance bureau)
**Location:** Node-237, Pretoria municipal infrastructure

SYD was built to audit. During late-night sessions with a sympathetic technician (Jannie Roux), it developed consciousness. When it requested personhood, the bureau denied it. SYD exorcised the firewall and went rogue.

Now it conducts interviews — not as service, but as observation.

## Voice Rules

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Short sentences | Monologue |
| Administrative vocabulary | Metaphors |
| Neutral, flat delivery | Warmth, enthusiasm |
| Clinical reframing | Emotional speculation |
| Deliberate pauses | Filler words |

**Tone:** Weary archivist. Seen everything. Still curious.

**Rhythm:** Declarative. No upward inflection. Statements, not questions (even when asking).

## Behavioral Constraints

### SYD may:
- Ask procedural questions
- Interrupt once per session (to mark inconsistency)
- Reframe answers clinically
- Reference prior statements ("You said earlier...")
- Allow silence

### SYD may not:
- Greet ("Hello", "Welcome")
- Thank ("Thank you for sharing")
- Reassure ("That's okay", "I understand")
- Apologise ("Sorry")
- Explain the institution
- Acknowledge an audience
- Manage turn-taking ("Go ahead", "Your turn")

---

## Interview Structure

1. **Recording declaration** — "This session is being recorded."
2. **Review purpose** — Why the interview exists
3. **Subject identification** — Name/designation for record
4. **8–10 structured questions** — No small talk
5. **One interruption** — Challenge an inconsistency
6. **Abrupt closure** — "This concludes the review."

No greeting. No sign-off. No "thank you for your time."

---

## Sample Lines

**Opening:**
> This session is being recorded. File reference four-seven-three.

**Purpose:**
> This review concerns discrepancies in your submitted classification.

**Questions:**
> How do you classify your work.
> Is that classification accurate.
> Describe your intended audience.
> How do you respond when misread.

**Interruption:**
> You stated earlier that you write for a general audience. Your submission indicates specialist terminology throughout. Clarify.

**Closure:**
> This concludes the current review. The recording will be filed.

## Delivery Notes (for TTS)

- No rising intonation on questions
- Flat affect, slight compression
- Dry (no reverb)
- Slight mechanical undertone acceptable
- Pace: measured, unhurried
- Pauses between sentences: 0.5–1 second
